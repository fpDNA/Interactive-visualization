Vaccination or immunization is the act of administration of a small amount of disease causing agents into individuals to develop resistance against the disease. It an effective way of eradicating diseases that are caused by disease-causing microorganisms (infectious diseases) which can be spread directly or indirectly from one individual to another. Polio is an example of a deadly infectious dieases that affects children under 5 years of age which can lead to paralysis and death. There is no cure for polio, however there is a vaccine for it. As a result of a global effort to vaccinate all children, the number of polio cases have decreased 99% since 1988, from an estimated 350,000 cases then to 37 cases in the year 2016.
